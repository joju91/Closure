# Efterplan — Design Context

## Design Context

### Users
Swedish adults handling practical affairs after a death — relatives, estate executors, next of kin. They come to Efterplan in a state of grief and cognitive overload. Their job to be done: get through the required practical steps without being overwhelmed. They need clarity, not decisions. They need to feel held, not lectured.

### Brand Personality
**3 words:** Warm, calm, trusted.

Efterplan should feel like a knowledgeable friend who has handled this before — steady, present, human. Not a corporate tool, not a SaaS product, not an insurance company. Like a trusted hand on the shoulder saying "here's what needs to happen, one step at a time."

**Tagline:** "För det som blir kvar när någon är borta" (For what remains when someone is gone)

**Emotional goal:** Reduce anxiety. Create a sense that this is manageable. Every design decision should lower cognitive load and increase feelings of safety and trust.

### Aesthetic Direction
**Warm & organic** — soft textures, warm neutrals, human and approachable. Not tech-y. Not clinical. Closer to premium Scandinavian editorial design than a startup landing page.

- Color: Deep slate blue (#2C4A6E) as the anchor — trustworthy, professional without coldness
- Background: Warm off-white (#F8F7F5) — never pure white, always tinted
- Typography: DM Sans — readable, warm, approachable. Could complement with a humanist serif for headings in future
- Spacing: Generous, breathing. Not cramped. Let content have room
- Border radius: Soft (12px) — rounded but not toy-like
- Theme: Light only. Warm off-white feel throughout

**Anti-references:**
- NOT dark/morbid — no black backgrounds, no cemetery aesthetics, no heavy themes that remind users of death
- NOT generic SaaS — no purple/teal gradients, no rocket-ship energy, no "growth tool" vibes
- NOT overly cheerful — no bright colors or playful illustrations that feel tone-deaf
- NOT insurance-stiff — no dense legal typography, bureaucratic coldness, or corporate distance

### Design Principles

1. **One thing at a time** — The interface should never overwhelm. Surface the most important thing, hide the rest. Deterministic UX: the system decides what's next.

2. **Warmth over polish** — Organic, human details beat cold precision. A slightly warm background, gentle shadows, and considered typography matter more than flashy effects.

3. **Trust through restraint** — Earn trust by removing noise, not adding badges and icons. White space communicates confidence. Clutter communicates anxiety.

4. **Clarity over completeness** — Not everything needs to be shown at once. Progressive disclosure is kind in this context. Let users discover depth as they're ready.

5. **Motion with care** — Transitions should be smooth and quiet. Nothing should pulse, bounce, or demand attention. Subtle entrance animations only. Respect prefers-reduced-motion.

### Tech Stack
- Vanilla JS, plain HTML, pure CSS — no build tools or frameworks
- Single-page app with three screens: Landing, Onboarding, Plan
- 20+ static SEO pages sharing the same stylesheet
- PWA (manifest + service worker)
- LocalStorage for state persistence
- Mobile-first, WCAG AA compliant
- Swedish language only
